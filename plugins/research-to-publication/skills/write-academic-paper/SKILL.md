# Write Academic Paper

Read `references/manuscript-contract.md` and use `assets/manuscript-outline.md`.

1. Confirm article type, target audience, author, length, and citation style.
2. Build the outline from approved claim IDs.
3. Draft each section with direct evidence before interpretation.
4. Label original synthesis and hypotheses as such.
5. Cite primary sources; cite NotebookLM only as a workflow artifact when methodologically relevant.
6. Audit every DOI, title, author, year, and strong numerical statement.
7. Check that limitations and counterevidence receive proportionate space.
8. Produce conceptual figures only when their relationships are supported by the synthesis.
9. Run a final claim-to-citation audit and report unresolved metadata.

Never invent evidence to fill narrative gaps. Downgrade the prose or expose the gap.
